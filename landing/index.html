WK Rotor Control

Point your antenna without leaving the chair

by IU2VWK

What it does

Built for the shack, tested on the air

Your QTH at the center of the world

Drop in your Maidenhead locator and the app pulls an azimuthal projection map straight from NS6T, centered on your station. No fiddling with PDF software, it converts to PNG by itself and overlays it under the compass.

See where you're actually pointing

The compass needle moves in real time as the rotor turns. Target heading on top, current position below. It just works, the way a rotor controller should, without making you read a manual first.

Talks GS-232, the way Yaesu intended

Plug your G-600 (or any GS-232 compatible rotor) into USB and the app handles the rest. Windows uses the browser's serial stack, macOS goes through a thin Python bridge. Same commands, same feel.

Try before you wire

No rotor connected? The simulation mode spins a virtual antenna so you can test headings, presets, and the map without touching a cable. Zero configuration, starts the moment you open the app.

How it works

Three steps from grid to heading

1

Enter locator

Type your Maidenhead grid square (like JN61fv). That's your station pinned on the map.

2

Generate map

Hit generate. NS6T sends back an azimuthal projection. The app converts it and slides it under the compass.

3

Turn the beam

Pick a preset or punch in a heading. The rotor swings around, the needle follows. Done.
